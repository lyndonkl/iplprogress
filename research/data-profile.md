# IPL / WPL Era Profile — Ground Truth

Computed 2026-07-03 from ball-by-ball Cricsheet JSON: 1243 men's IPL matches (2008-2026), 88 WPL matches (2023-2026). Seasons normalized: 2007/08->2008, 2009/10->2010, 2020/21->2020. Super-over innings excluded from all ball-level stats.

## 10 most striking shifts

1. **The scoring explosion is recent and still accelerating.** Men's IPL avg full first-innings score sat near 158 for 2008-12, drifted to ~172 by 2018, then leapt to 183 (2023), 190 (2024), 196 (2026). Overall run rate: 7.90 (2008-12) -> 9.88 (2026).
2. **200+ totals went from freak to routine.** 200+ innings per season: ~6 in 2008-12, 18 in 2022, then 37 (2023) -> 41 (2024) -> 65 (2026). 240+ innings: 6 total in 2008-2023 combined vs 29 in 2024-26 alone.
3. **The six-hitting revolution is aerial, not along the ground.** Sixes per match: 10.7 (2008) -> 19.3 (2026), balls-per-six from 21 to 12; fours per match barely moved (29.4 -> 31.5). Boundary share of runs: 51% (2009 low) -> 65% (2026).
4. **The powerplay became the most-changed phase.** PP run rate rose 7.45 -> 9.14 (2008-12 vs 2022-26, +1.7), more than middle (7.45 -> 8.74) or death (9.46 -> 10.78). By 2026 the powerplay (10.10) nearly matches the death overs (10.79) — teams now attack from ball one.
5. **Dot balls are dying while wickets are NOT rising to match.** Dot-ball %: 37.5% (2008-12) -> 33.8% (2022-26, low 32.3% in 2025). Wickets per match moved only 11.8 -> 12.2 — the extra aggression has been nearly free.
6. **Toss strategy flipped to chase-first — but chasing stopped paying.** Toss winners chose to field 49% of the time in 2008-12 vs an average 76% since 2018 (2025: 81%, 2026: 82%). Yet chase win % shows no era trend (65% peak in 2016, 45% in 2023), and toss winners win the match only ~52% of the time overall.
7. **How batters get out has transformed.** Caught share of dismissals: 61% (2008-12) -> 72% (2022-26, 77% in 2026); bowled fell 19% -> 14%; run outs halved from 11% to 5% — fitness/fielding standards and the death of the risky single.
8. **Bowling discipline is getting worse, not better.** Wides+no-balls per match fell from 9.3 (2008) to 7.1 (2013), then climbed steadily to 11.6 (2026) — consistent with bowlers chasing wide-yorker/short-ball extremes against modern hitting.
9. **The Impact Player rule (2023+) is near-universal and coincides with the scoring leap.** 74/74 matches used it in 2023 and adoption stayed ~100% (2026: 73/74). Avg 1st-innings score rose +25 runs from 2022 to 2026. DRS reviews run ~2/match since 2022 (introduced 2018 at 1.4/match).
10. **WPL is a different scoring dialect, converging fast.** WPL avg 1st-inn: 157 (2023) -> 169 (2026); RR 8.08 -> 8.54 — near men's 2022 level after four seasons. WPL hits half the sixes (8.0/match vs IPL 2022-26 16.8) but MORE fours (36.4 vs 29.9), reviews more per match (2.3 vs 1.9), and its toss went ~100% field-first by 2025-26.

## Notes and caveats

- "Full" 1st-innings average excludes no-results, D/L-method matches, and matches where the chase target was set for fewer than 20 overs. Sample size per season shown implicitly by match counts; a handful of matches drop out in rainy seasons.
- Dot ball = legal delivery conceding zero total runs. Balls-per-boundary uses legal balls only. Boundary detection uses runs.batter in {4,6} minus the rare non_boundary (all-run) flag.
- DRS review objects appear in the data from 2018 (men) — zero before that reflects DRS not existing in the IPL, not missing data.
- Impact Player replacements (reason=impact_player) exist 2023+ in men's IPL only; WPL has no Impact Player rule (any stray replacement entries are concussion subs).
- Distinct players counted by registry ID where available (starting XIs + named subs in the players lists).
- 2008 season label in raw data is "2007/08", 2010 is "2009/10", 2020 is "2020/21" (COVID season played Sep-Nov 2020 in UAE).
