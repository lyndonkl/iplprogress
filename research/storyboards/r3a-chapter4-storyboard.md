### 0.1 Encoding grammar — the tide skyline (cognitive-design)

- **Height IS the argument.** Ch 4's controlling layout, **`tide`**, condenses every first-innings ball to a **column** whose **height = the total its team finished on**. Columns are grouped left-to-right by season (2008 on the left, this year on the right), and within a season they are packed side by side, sorted short-to-tall, so each season reads as a little skyline and the whole field reads as a coastline rising as your eye travels right. Every ball of one first innings stacks on that innings' single column, so a busy season is a dense skyline and the height is read directly, not eyeballed off a scatter. This is the single most important encoding in the chapter and the reason the morph is worth its budget: two decades of "teams score more now" becomes one rising coastline you can see in a glance.
- **Only BETWEEN-season x means anything (time); WITHIN-season x is just rank.** Left-to-right ACROSS seasons is time, and it is the only x-reading the copy explains. Left-to-right INSIDE a season is pure sort-rank (shortest to tallest) and carries no meaning, so to stop a reader inferring a spurious "left = early games" trend (or reading the drowned short columns clustering left as a within-season slump), the layout **suppresses all within-season x ticks and gridlines** and keeps each season block **visually bounded** (a faint season separator), and C4-2 step 1 discloses the rank-packing in a half-clause. The only labeled x marks are the season boundaries (2008 … now).
- **Every ball is still on screen.** Only **full first innings** build columns (20 overs bowled or all out). Second innings, super-over, and rain-shortened balls settle into a **faint all-time reservoir haze** behind and below the skyline, so "every ball ever bowled is here" stays literally true while the columns carry the argument. The reservoir never re-sorts and is never captioned as a column; it is texture, and the footnote (C4-2) names exactly what is in it and why.
- **The waterline is a lookup, drawn on the annotation plane and mirrored as a field state.** The **going rate** (the score that wins exactly half the time, per season) is a horizontal blue line, the **waterline**, rising left-to-right across the seasons. Its per-season height comes **precomputed at build time from engine #1** (`par.json`, a lookup, per the standing rule); the client interpolates between precomputed per-season par heights as it scrubs and never fits a model. A column whose top sits **below** the current waterline is **drowned** (dimmed one-plus stop toward the reservoir); a column standing **above** it stays lit and dry. The drowning is **luminance only** (a dimmed column), never a hue change: the waterline LINE is the only blue thing on screen, an annotation-plane element, so hue stays identity everywhere (team color, WPL family). The 200 line and the 230 line are the same kind of fixed annotation-plane reference marks.
- **The skyline never re-sorts; the rise is real data.** Every innings lands at a **fixed** column (its total does not change once the innings is over, and its season slot is fixed). The reader scrubs a season pointer and the **waterline rises** (the going rate genuinely climbs), the **share of dry columns above the 200 line grows** (real season shares from `scenes/ch4.json`), and the whole coastline is visibly taller on the right because later seasons genuinely finished on bigger totals. The columns do not move. The water moves, and different columns light or drown. This honors "every ball on screen" (the fixed skyline + reservoir) while the waterline and the season shares carry the argument, exactly as Ch 3 held the frontier plane and scrubbed a season pointer.
- **Working-memory cap holds:** ≤3 numbers per caption step, one on-screen change per step. The rising going rate (par) and the 200-club share never share a caption step (two arguments, told one at a time). The 2023 jump is its own beat and carries no cause.

### 0.2 Register — THE RISING TIDE (on-screen copy rules, Ch 4)

Standing-back awe at how far the water rose, with a note of unease underneath it. Ch 1 was the thesis, Ch 2 mourned the anchor, Ch 3 admired the bowler; Ch 4's subject is neither batter nor bowler, it is **the ground everyone stands on**, and it moved. Four honesty guardrails, all load-bearing:

1. **The subject is the environment, not a hero.** The chapter never re-credits the batter (Ch 1-2) or the bowler (Ch 3). No "batters got braver" beats here. The grammatical subject of the tide beats is the water, the going rate, the ground, the record book. The vertigo is the point: a score that would have won now loses, and nobody on the field decided that on any single day.
2. **The 2023 mystery is genuinely deferred.** C4-5 shows the cliff in full drama and then refuses to explain it. **No scene in this chapter names a cause** (not the Impact Player rule, not new personnel, not the skill curve). "Something snapped in 2023. Hold that thought. The answer is three chapters away." is authored copy, and the footnote layer here carries only *how the number is computed*, never *why it moved*. Ch 7 delivers the partial answer; Ch 10 the verdict.
3. **The WPL is never behind.** The WPL beat (C4-10) is two clocks in one beat, per the house rule: one dial placed on the IPL timeline (its 200 club lands between IPL 2008 and 2015) and, in the same breath, the clock that refuses the timeline (at the same league age its tide rises faster, and it rises four-led, along the ground not over the rope). "Still an event" and "its own flood" are the grammatical subjects, never a deficit.
4. **The venue twist is contrarian, said plainly.** The flat-pitch era did NOT flatten the country; grounds are pulling apart. This is the surprising thing, so say it straight (voice rule 3), never "you might think X, but actually not-X" cleverness dressed up. Bangalore is the flood plain, Chennai keeps its character, and both are true in the same era.

### Scene C4-4 — The 200 club (hero: Threshold Exceedance Curves)

**Annotation plane:**
- The **200 line** bright and labeled "200", the one line the copy names. **Caption steps (one change each):**
