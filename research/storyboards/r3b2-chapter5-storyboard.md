### 0.1 Encoding grammar — the over rail, the worm, and the worth grid (cognitive-design)

- **The over rail (C5-2/C5-3).** The set piece's six deliveries lift out of the dimmed field and fly to six fixed **rail slots** across the middle of the viewport, left to right in bowling order. The GL points fly; at each slot a **DOM ball chip** takes over (hybrid contract: names and numbers are DOM), carrying batter · bowler · what happened · the swing. The rest of the field dims hard behind (set-piece dimming; nav dims per the shell's existing rule). This is a subset-highlight, not a re-sort: six points move, 316,193 hold.
- **The worm is felt before any formula.** Beneath the rail, on the annotation plane, the **worm** draws left to right as the scrub advances: one line whose height is *how often the chasing side wins from that exact spot, out of a hundred* (the interlude's Meter B frame, locked to "in 100"). The worm is the chapter's teaching image and it appears BEFORE any price board or grid. Its seven anchor values (before ball 1 … after ball 6) are **pipeline-emitted lookups** in `scenes/ch5.json`, never client-fit (§6, open call b): the shipped `wp_grid` resolves whole overs only, so within-over states come from a pooled **endgame ladder** built the same honest way (observed chase outcomes at late-chase states, evidence counts carried, thin states pooled by rule and flagged). The final anchor is pinned to the result (Chennai 0 in 100). The worm's y axis is labeled at 0, 50, and 100 only.
- **The worth grid (C5-5 onward), the controlling layout `worth`.** Every ball flies to the cell of the **match situation it was bowled in**: x = the over of the innings (1 left → 20 right), y = wickets fallen when the ball was bowled (0 at the top → 9 at the bottom). Cell membership comes from one new per-point byte, `statecell.u8` (= over×10 + wicketsFallen, 0..199; sentinel 255 routes the 189 super-over deliveries to a tiny margin haze so "every ball on screen" stays literally true). Positions are computed in-shader (cell + deterministic jitter packing); **no positions cross the wire**.
- **Brightness IS the price.** Each cell's luminance encodes the expected runs still to come from that state, read from `re288.json` for the displayed era band; the whole recolor is driven by a 200-entry lookup table passed as a uniform/texture. **Density normalization (binding):** perceived cell brightness is point density × per-point luminance, and cell populations vary enormously (over 1 with none down holds nearly every innings; the deep late rows are sparse), so uncorrected per-point luminance would make apparent brightness partly encode frequency, not price. The table therefore carries each cell's point count alongside its price, and the shader scales per-point intensity by a per-cell normalization gain (target integrated brightness ÷ cellCount, clamped), so a cell's INTEGRATED brightness tracks the price table no matter how many balls live there. A dense cheap cell must never outshine a sparse expensive one; §4 asserts the rendered rank-order in a dense and a sparse cell under every table. **The era flip (2008-10 → 2023-26) and the difference lens move COLOR ONLY; positions never change after the morph lands.** The grid does not move. The prices move. (Exactly the Ch 4 discipline: fixed columns, moving water.) Luminance only, hue stays identity (team color, WPL family); the difference lens encodes the SIZE of the rise as luminance, unsigned, and the channel's change of meaning is re-glossed on screen the moment it lands (persistent state label + legend swap + a dip-to-dark re-light, C5-6a), with the pipeline QA asserting the rise is non-negative in every cell the copy points at (§4). Masked WPL cells are **hatched on the annotation plane** above the grid, never a hue and never a dimness that could read as a low price.
- **State honesty.** The grid places every delivery, first innings and chases alike, by its real (over, wickets) state; the price tags are the first-innings "what a batting side usually still gets" surfaces, exactly the number the interlude's runs meter already applied to chase spots, and the footnote (C5-5) discloses this in the same plain words the interlude used. Chases get their own currency everywhere it matters: the worm, the finisher beat, and the payoff card all read the win grid.
- **Working-memory cap holds:** ≤3 numbers per caption step, one on-screen change per step. The two currencies never share a caption step without both being glossed (win reads are always "N in 100"; run reads are always "runs").
