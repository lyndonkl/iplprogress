## Hard invariants (the header block, every scene inherits these; none may break them)

1. **ONE controlling morph.** Free field to the **chronological ribbon** (`ribbon`, C10-2), plus its free reverse leg (C10-9, which returns to the cold-open free field, so the piece ends where it began). That is the chapter's entire morph budget. Nothing else re-sorts the field.
2. **The ribbon is a PURE FUNCTION of `position.x`, so it adds NO new per-point buffer and NO new per-point attribute (release-blocking-safe).** `position.x` already holds the chronological point index (0..316198, in flatten season-blocked order, which is essentially match order 2008 to 2026), and `uInvN` already exists. The ribbon x is `(position.x * uInvN * 2 - 1) * uHalfW * 0.95` and its y is a thin band (`uRibbonBandY` plus jitter). **No `.u16` ships, no data texture is baked, no 15th attribute is bound. The field STAYS at 14 attributes.** A 15th attribute blacked out the whole field on one build (field integration map, CONTRACT §24.2), so this is not optional; the ribbon is the simplest morph in the whole piece.
3. **Three held field scalars over the morph, no second re-sort.** `teleportProgress` (0 to 1, the Teleporter lift engage), `teleportLift` (the world-space height the picked player's balls rise out of the ribbon), and `teleportOthersDim` (a luminance multiplier that sinks every non-picked ball while one player is aloft). All are lerps over the held ribbon, NOT morphs, and all default inert so every prior release renders byte-identically.
4. **The 2D annotation-plane scenes.** The **seismograph strip** (C10-3), the **fault map subway** (C10-4), the **verdict card** (C10-5), the **Teleporter card** (C10-6), and the **convergence fans** (C10-7) are DOM/SVG over the held (dimmed) ribbon. The payoff (C10-8) is DOM.
5. **The fault-line cracks are SCENE-DRAWN SVG, and the strictness dial is a PRECOMPUTED-SEGMENTATION LOOKUP (no field work, no re-fit).** The field never draws a crack. The scene reads `getRibbonLayout()` (`{ box, pointToX(i) }`), draws vertical SVG marks at `pointToX(breakIndex)`, and encodes each crack's certainty (how sure we are) on TWO redundant honest channels: the glow/opacity of a soft-edged band (its intensity honestly tracks the emitted posterior, and its width honestly tracks the location-uncertainty, so a fuzzy break reads as "unsure where, too") AND a discrete style (surer = solid full-height stroke, less sure = dashed and shorter). To clear the detection floor without touching the certainty signal, every crack ALSO carries a separate always-visible high-contrast locator stroke (a thin neutral line with a 1px dark halo so it pops preattentively off the identity-hued ribbon) and a **direct year label in a tick-lane above the band** (direct labels beat legends and anchor the position read even when the certainty glow is faint). The scene also tints the segments BETWEEN cracks as faint alternating era-bands and shows a live **"N eras" counter** on the dial (external memory, never make the reader count). Dragging the strictness dial swaps which precomputed break-index set the scene draws (β0.3 to β14, giving 6 down to 1 segments), and adjacent era-bands visibly FUSE as the count drops; **it is a pure lookup into `scenes/ch10.json`, never a live re-fit.** The locator stroke and label never encode certainty and so are never "brightening a faint break for legibility"; the certainty stays honestly on the glow + solid/dashed channels and is never inflated.
6. **The Teleporter lift is a SUBSET on the spare bit of `aRunOut`, NOT a new buffer.** `aRunOut` is a packed byte (bit0 run-out from Ch2, bit1 spark from Ch7); Chapter 10 never co-renders with Ch2 or Ch7, so `setTeleport(indices)` (cloned from `setSparks`) writes the spare **bit2** (`arr[i] &= 0xFB; arr[i] |= 4`), read in-shader as `(int(aRunOut + 0.5) & 4) != 0`. No per-point attribute is added; the field STAYS at 14 attributes.
7. **Payload budget.** `scenes/ch10.json` plus its inline 16 payoff cards ship well inside the **2MB-per-chapter gzip budget**. No new per-point buffer at all (Ch 10 is buffer-free, like Ch 6 and Ch 7).
8. **Reduced motion = live end-state jump-cuts.** Every scene renders its landed state instantly, no motion or tap required. The morph forces t=1; **the ribbon renders as the strictness-dial stills (the 2-era, 4-era, and 6-era cracked ribbons drawn as static frames, defaulting to the middle 4-era still), with the era-bands tinted and the "N eras" counter shown**; the seismograph strip, the fault map, the verdict card, the convergence fans, and the payoff render as static end states; **the Teleporter is a CARD, not a motion beat, under reduced motion, and it renders as TWO hard-separated sub-cards (Machine A: the translate-one-player naive-ghost-versus-honest-re-quote markers on one aligned strike-rate axis, with no lift; then Machine B: the rank-against-your-own-era bar-swap), with Machine A's 214/224 figures cleared before Machine B's +57/+35 world is shown**.
9. **Glossary rule.** No statistical term of art appears in main-flow or teaser copy. Every device carries a fan-language stage name on screen; the technical name lives one click deep in the footnote (§0.2). It is "where the game broke," never "changepoint"; "how sure we are," never "posterior"; "the strictness dial," never "the penalty"; "where he ranked that year," never "percentile"; "how far above the going rate of his time," never "SR+" and **never a z-score**.
10. **The WPL framing rule.** The WPL is never "behind." In Chapter 10 it is **a young league whose future you can already clock**: its run rate is rising toward where the men's game sits today on a slope you can read, and its six-hitting is so far out on four seasons that it is honestly off the clock. Uncertainty is owned loudly (fans, not prophecy). Banned as an object of "behind / lags / catching up / years behind / immature." (The comparison is pinned to a FIXED present-day level rather than the moving, faster-rising men's line, so no gap-narrowing is over-claimed, fallacies-guard S4.)
11. **Evidence, not proof; the data draws its own lines (the integrity spine).** The chapter's whole move is to STOP narrating eras and let the segmentation draw the fault lines itself, then own that the evidence is modest (the break posteriors run 0.15 to 0.44). The 2023 verdict is "three suspects, all guilty on different counts," a weight of evidence across three chapters, never "the rule CAUSED the explosion." The convergence clock is an extrapolation with an owned band, never a prophecy.
12. **Artifact wins.** Every {braced} literal and every on-screen number traces to a `scenes/ch10.json` field; the emitted value wins over any teaser here if rounding or definition differs.

### 0.1 Encoding grammar, the ribbon, the cracks, the strictness dial, the fault map, the verdict card, the Teleporter card, the convergence fan (cognitive-design)

- **The unit is unchanged, a dot is still one ball, and the reader watches balls sort themselves into TIME (the controlling morph).** In `free`, the field is 316,199 balls scattered in the sky. In `ribbon`, every ball flies to its place in one long horizontal band, ordered left to right by when it was bowled: the leftmost dot is the first ball of 2008, the rightmost is the last ball of 2026. So the reader keeps the "a dot is a ball" contract from Chapter 1 and learns one new mark on top of it: **left to right is time, and the whole history is one ribbon.** The morph is the simplest in the piece, a pure function of `position.x` (invariant 2). The ribbon is taught in C10-2 before any argument, because "the x axis is time and every dot is a ball in the order it was bowled" is the one mark the entire chapter rests on.

- **The ribbon is spaced by BALLS, not by calendar years, and the copy owns that so no false trend is read off its shape (encoding integrity, fallacies-guard).** Because x is the chronological ball index, every equal slice of width holds an equal number of balls, so the band's brightness is held UNIFORM across its length (there is no rightward brightening the eye could misread as "the game intensifying over time"). The trade is that seasons are unequal width: recent seasons have more matches, so they occupy MORE of the ribbon, and 2008 is compressed into a narrow strip on the left. That widening on the right is a fact about how many matches are played now, **not** a change in the pace of the game, and the persistent legend and the C10-2 footnote say so in one plain line ("the right side is wider because there are more matches now, not because anything sped up"). One consequence carried forward: because recent seasons are wide but their year-cracks are only a season apart, the three modern cracks (2022 / 2023 / 2024) sit within a few percent of width of each other, so the fine modern ORDER is read on the fault map (C10-4, whose axis is equal-spaced by year), not off the ribbon.

- **The fault-line cracks are vertical breaks in the ribbon, drawn where the game changed, and their certainty is carried on two redundant honest channels (scene-drawn SVG, invariant 5).** In C10-3 the scene draws vertical marks straight down through the ribbon at the seasons the data says the game broke. **A crack is NOT authored by hand and NOT placed on a round year for tidiness; it sits at the exact ball-position the segmentation found.** How sure we are (the emitted posterior, 0.15 to 0.44, so most breaks are modest) is drawn on TWO channels at once, because opacity alone is near the bottom of the encoding hierarchy and a 1px line at 0.15 alpha over 316,199 glowing dots is below the reliable detection floor on a bright phone: (i) a **soft-edged glow band** whose intensity honestly tracks the posterior and whose width honestly tracks how unsure we are of WHERE the break sits (fuzzy = "unsure where, too"), and (ii) a **discrete style** (surer = a solid, full-height stroke; less sure = dashed and shorter). So "how sure" survives on a second, better channel even when the glow is faint, and we never brighten a faint break to imply more confidence than the data carries. Legibility of the MARK (not its certainty) is guaranteed separately: each crack carries an always-visible thin **neutral high-contrast locator stroke with a 1px dark halo** so it pops preattentively off the identity-hued ribbon, and a **direct year label in a tick-lane above the band** so the position read is anchored even when the certainty glow is near zero. **Division of labour (perceptual resolution):** the ribbon carries only the read it can actually resolve, the BIG gap ("sixes cracked way back in the mid-2010s; everything else is piled at the right edge"); the fine modern order (wides 2022 / scoring 2023 / everything 2024) is delegated to the fault map (C10-4), whose year axis is equal-spaced and where that order resolves. The cracks are the record's honesty made visible: the game did not break once, it broke in stages.

- **The strictness dial merges the ERAS (the tinted regions), and 6 eras loosen to 2 (a precomputed lookup, invariant 5).** A single slider labelled **"how big a change counts as a new era"** rides under the ribbon. To make "eras merge" a percept and not an inference, the intended mental model (segments fusing) is drawn literally: the segments BETWEEN cracks are tinted as faint alternating era-bands, so an era is an actual visible region, not just the gap between two lines. At its loosest, the ribbon carries the full staggered set of cracks (six era-bands, split at 2009, 2010, 2014, then again at 2023 and 2024). Drag it stricter and the fainter cracks wink out AND their two adjacent era-bands visibly FUSE into one, so the ribbon collapses region by region, six eras to four to three to **two**, until only the one dominant modern break at 2023 is left standing between two eras. A live **"N eras" counter** on the dial reads out the current count (external memory, the reader never counts bands by eye). **The dial does not re-fit anything; each stop reads a precomputed break-index set out of `scenes/ch10.json`** (the penalty sweep β0.3 = 6 segments, β0.6 = 4, β1.0 = 3, β4.0 = 2, β14+ = 1, all verified exactly). On mobile the scripted scroll steps the merge through 2 or 3 discrete stops inside the read-gap (the stack collapsing step by step is the point of step 5), never one 6-to-2 jump. The point the reader takes is that the modern game is a stack: loosen your eyes and you see a decade of staggered revolutions; squint hard and they collapse into the one big 2023 fault. Both readings are true, and the dial lets the reader hold both.

- **The seismograph strip is the pulse under the crack, a small stacked line chart (C10-3, 2D SVG).** Beside the cracked ribbon, a compact stack of small sparklines shows the actual metric series the cracks come from: the six-rate climbing, the run rate climbing, the wide rate stepping up, each drawn 2008 to 2026 with its own break marked. It is the evidence panel, so the crack on the ribbon is never an assertion the reader has to take on faith: they can see the six-rate genuinely step at 2014, the run rate genuinely step at 2023. The load-bearing "the break is real" read is a **position-on-a-common-scale** read (the line visibly steps), never a color read; each mini-line is direct-labelled ("how often a ball is a six," "runs an over") so the strip needs no legend. Because the hero already teaches four new devices (crack, era-band, dial, and this second chart type), the strip and the ribbon never compete for figure-ground: the strip stays small and subordinate, and when it is the focus of a step the ribbon dims further so one thing is spotlit at a time (the chapter's `dim` discipline already supports this); on mobile it is revealed as the pulse-under-the-crack step, not co-displayed the whole scene.

- **The fault map is a subway map, metrics as lines, break years as stations, on an equal-spaced YEAR axis (C10-4, 2D SVG).** Each metric (sixes, scoring, wides, dots, boundaries) is drawn as one horizontal subway line running 2008 to 2026 with years equally spaced, so this is where the fine modern order the ribbon cannot resolve is actually read. Each line carries a **station dot at every year it broke**. When two or more metrics broke in the same year, their stations sit at a shared **interchange drawn as a single ringed "big-station" glyph** (real transit convention), so "many lines meet here" reads preattentively rather than as a blob of co-located dots (2023 is the big interchange, where scoring, boundaries and dots all broke together; 2024 is a second interchange for sixes, scoring, wides and boundaries). The two load-bearing lines, **sixes and scoring, are drawn ADJACENT** (so the only thing the eye tracks is the horizontal gap between them, with no vertical eye-travel). The load-bearing read, "sixes broke about five years before scoring did," is a **horizontal-distance** read (the sixes line has its main station at 2014-2018, the scoring line has its at 2023, and the gap between them is visible), colorblind-robust because it is distance, not hue. The reveal is tied to the scroll STEP, not an optional tap: the two hero lines draw for the distance beat, then the boundaries and dots lines and the ringed 2023 glyph reveal for the interchange beat, so a one-thumb scroll lands both. The subway metaphor is taught before it is argued: the caption says what a line, a station and an interchange are before naming any gap.

- **The verdict card closes the case as three CO-EQUAL panels, never a partitioned bar (C10-5, 2D DOM/SVG).** After the bridge-player evidence, a single card names the three suspects behind the 2023-24 explosion and stamps each **guilty on a different count**, with the chapter that caught it. It is drawn as **three distinct, co-equal panels, NOT a stacked bar or pie** (a partition would falsely imply the three sum to 100% and invite the "so what share was the rule?" inference the evidence cannot answer). Each panel is labelled by its EVIDENCE TYPE and carries its chapter badge: **an enabling condition** (the rule opened the door, Chapter 7), **a measured share** (new faces walked through, about two-thirds of the 2023-24 step, quantified here), and **a long-run trend** (the skill curve had been climbing for a decade, Chapter 1). Only the middle panel carries a number, because only turnover is quantified. The legitimate 1/3-vs-2/3 split (within-player vs turnover) stays a two-way bar in step 3; the three-SUSPECT card is never partitioned. It is a case-closed card, not a causal claim: the register (§0.2) states plainly that this is a weight of evidence across three chapters, three things that each did some of the work, never one cause that "made" the explosion happen (guardrail 2 bans "made / caused / drove / inevitable" here).

- **The Teleporter is TWO hard-separated machines on one scene, never co-displayed (C10-6, a field-subset lift plus 2D cards).** The two machines answer two different questions with two different number-worlds and colliding units, so they are drawn as two sub-cards with distinct layouts and colour-worlds, each with its own orient beat, and **the first machine's figures are cleared from screen before the second machine's world appears** (a reader holding "214 vs 224" must never be handed "+57% vs +35%" at the same time). On mobile they are separate scroll sub-segments, one machine per viewport.
  - **Machine A, translate ONE player through time (unit = strike rate).** The reader picks a player-season (Sehwag 2008 by default, or their team legend). That player's real balls **lift out of the ribbon** as a small bright cluster (the field subset, invariant 6) so the reader sees the actual innings being re-quoted. An era dial drags the year forward, and the re-quote is drawn as **two markers on ONE aligned vertical strike-rate axis anchored at his real 2008 number (185)**, so the gap is a SEEN vertical distance, not an arithmetic subtraction: **the honest re-quote** (hold the player at the same rank he actually reached, ~214, a solid marker with an uncertainty band) and **the naive ghost** (just scale the old number up by how much faster the league scores now, ~224, a pale dotted ghost marker that overshoots), with the gap between them shaded. **The shaded gap IS the exhibit**: the ghost overshoots because it forgets the player was already an outlier, and you cannot just scale an outlier up. **Integrity check (release-blocking):** the honest marker's uncertainty band must NOT reach the ghost, i.e. the emitted band half-width must be strictly less than the ~10-point gap, or the ghost sits inside the honest band and "overshoot" is perceptually and statistically false; QA asserts band < gap on the emitted numbers.
  - **Machine B, rank each player against his OWN era (unit = percent above par), drawn as a bar-SWAP.** After Machine A is cleared, a different question: who was furthest ahead of the going rate of his own time? Each innings is measured against the going rate of its OWN year, drawn as two panels that physically swap. Panel 1 (raw strike rate): Fraser-McGurk's bar is taller. Panel 2 (measured against each era's going rate, with each era's par drawn as the baseline, low-par 2011 vs high-par 2024): the bars **swap**, and Gayle's is now taller. The swap animation carries the whole "raw says Fraser-McGurk, era-adjusted says Gayle" reversal as a percept, with no subtraction, and never as a z-score (a plain percent gap, §0.2). The naive/honest split and the rank-vs-raw split are each taught before they are argued; the machine is a lookup, never a live model (every re-quote is precomputed in `scenes/ch10.json`).

- **The convergence clock is solid history, then a fan, and the crossing is read as a WINDOW on the time axis (C10-7, 2D SVG, small multiples).** Each metric is drawn as a **solid measured line up to 2026, a salient vertical TODAY marker, and only THEN a fan** (a central dashed line with a widening, gradient-faded shaded band), so it reads as "history, then forecast," not a broken chart floating in space, and the band's soft edges (Bank-of-England convention) read as "the spread of possible futures," not an error bar on a line. The three forecasts are drawn as **small multiples** (men's run rate / WPL run rate / WPL six-hitting, side by side on desktop, stacked on mobile) rather than one overlaid tangle, so each reads cleanly and the WPL keeps its distinct teal identity with no phantom overlap regions. The load-bearing "the men's game crosses ten an over" read is drawn as an **interval on the time axis**: the "10 an over" gridline is directly labelled, the crossing point is marked, and the band's intersection with it is drawn as a **bracket on the time axis ("somewhere in here, 2027-2031")**, a range the reader already understands, rather than asking the reader to eyeball band-width. The WPL run-rate fan is drawn **honestly wider** because four seasons buy little confidence; the WPL six-hitting fan is rendered as a DELIBERATE annotation, not a chart that failed: a flat central line with its target level pushed OFF-SCREEN behind an arrow and a "not foreseeable on four seasons" label, so an un-resolving fan reads as a chosen honest statement (and never as the banned "WPL is hopeless / behind" frame). A fan, not a line, is the honest encoding for an extrapolation (fallacies-guard: a single confident line would over-claim precision the four-season and eighteen-season records do not have).

- **One label for one concept.** The whole picture is "the ribbon" (and one dot is still "a ball") after C10-2. A vertical break is "a crack" or "a fault line," and the dial is "the strictness dial." The subway lines are "the fault map." The re-quoting machine is "the Teleporter," its two figures are "the naive ghost" and "the honest re-quote," and the era-relative face is "how far above his own era." The forecast is "the convergence clock." The WPL is "the young league whose future you can already clock," never "behind."

### 0.2 Register, THE SYNTHESIS (case closed, honestly) (on-screen copy rules, Ch 10)

The chapter's voice is **the synthesis, and it is valedictory**. This is the finale: it stops narrating eras and lets the data draw its own fault lines, closes the mystery the piece has carried since Chapter 4, then exhales back to where it began. The register is confident (the machine reads the whole history at once) but scrupulously honest (the evidence is modest and it says so), and it lands with a quiet valedictory close (the piece ends where it began, except now you can read it). Five guardrails, on screen in every scene:

1. **The data draws the lines, we do not (the integrity spine, release-blocking).** The chapter's move is to hand the pen to the segmentation: it says "here is where the data itself says the game broke," and then it OWNS that the evidence is modest ("the cracks are faint because the data is only somewhat sure"). It never dresses a modest break up as a certainty. "The data breaks here / the record draws a line / where the game broke" are the honest verbs; "proves / definitely / the era began exactly in" are banned.

2. **Case closed means the evidence weighs one way, never proof (the integrity spine, release-blocking).** The 2023 verdict is three suspects, each guilty on a different count, a weight of evidence gathered across three chapters. The chapter never says the rule "caused" the explosion, never says new faces "made" scoring jump, never says the skill curve "drove" it. "The rule opened the door / new faces walked through / the skill had been climbing / about two-thirds of the jump was" are the honest verbs; "caused / because of / made / drove / proves" as causal assertions are banned in main flow.

3. **The WPL is a young league whose future you can already clock, never behind (the house WPL framing rule, made literal).** C10-7 shows the WPL run rate rising toward where the men's game sits today on a slope you can read, and its six-hitting so far out on four seasons that it is honestly off the clock. That is framed as **a young league you can already forecast**, with the uncertainty owned loudly (fans, not prophecy). "Behind / catching up / years behind / developing toward / immature / a different animal" are banned, and "closing on the men's game" is avoided because it implies gap-narrowing against a moving, faster-rising target (fallacies-guard S4); the WPL is "young," "rising," "a league whose run rate you can already clock," compared to a fixed present-day level.

4. **Orient every exhibit before you argue with it.** Nobody is asked to read a ribbon, a crack, a strictness dial, a subway map, a verdict card, a re-quoting machine, or a forecast fan until the mark has been taught in dead-plain words: what a dot and the time axis are (C10-2), what a crack and its brightness mean and what the dial does (C10-3), what a subway line and a station mean (C10-4), what the three suspects are (C10-5), what the naive ghost and the honest re-quote are (C10-6), what a fan and its widening band mean (C10-7). The ribbon gets the most patient orientation because the whole chapter is argued on its one time axis.

5. **One number per idea; the honest deltas ship straight.** Each caption step carries one moving number (a before-and-after pair counts as one idea). The full per-season metric ladders, the penalty sweep, the posteriors, the shift-share components, the era-translation factors, and the convergence fit live in the footnotes, never on the main-flow card. **The six honest deltas ship straight and are never fudged toward the teaser** (§3): sixes broke 2014 then 2018 (not one clean 2018), about two-thirds turnover (not three-quarters), naive ceiling about 224 (not 228), "how far above his own era" as a percent-above-par gap and never a z-score, and the WPL six-hitting owned as off the clock.

**Banned on screen** (main flow plus teasers), inheriting every prior list plus Ch 10's terms of art: *changepoint, change-point, changepoint detection, PELT, BOCPD, penalty, segmentation, segment, product-partition, posterior, prior (as a term of art), Bayesian, credible interval, z-score, z, standard deviation, sigma, percentile (as a bare term of art), quantile, SR+ (as a bare term), strike-rate-plus, par (as a bare term of art), shift-share, decomposition, within-player, between-player, turnover (as a bare term of art), counterfactual, extrapolation, linear fit, regression, slope (as a bare term of art), trend line (as a term of art), confidence interval, confidence band, forecast (as a bare term of art), autocorrelation, permutation.* Also inherited and still banned as league comparisons: *behind, ahead (of a league), lags, lagging, catching up, N years behind, developing toward, immature, a different animal.* (Note: "run rate closes first, six-hitting closes last" describes two metrics WITHIN the WPL and is ALLOWED; only "behind / lags" as a league-standing comparison is banned. ## 10. The Player Teleporter card (scene-authored, from `scenes/ch10.json`)

C10-6 (and the C10-8 payoff row 2) open the Teleporter when a player-season is picked and the era dial is dragged. It is scene-authored DOM/SVG (the field only lifts the subset), rendered from the precomputed per-player-season table, so it holds no rAF loop and spends no set-piece budget. It is TWO hard-separated machines with distinct layouts and colour-worlds, never co-displayed; the first machine's figures clear before the second's world appears; on mobile they are separate scroll sub-segments, one machine per viewport (cognitive-design #4, #15).

- **Machine A, translate one player through time (unit = strike rate):**
  - **The lift:** the picked player-season's real balls lift out of the ribbon via `setTeleport(indices)` (the spare `aRunOut` bit2), so the reader sees the actual innings the machine is re-quoting sitting above the band; every other ball dims (`teleportOthersDim`).
  - **The aligned axis (not two floating figures):** ONE vertical strike-rate axis, anchored at his real 2008 number (185), so every re-quote is a SEEN height above the anchor, not an arithmetic subtraction (cognitive-design #5). **The honest re-quote** is a solid marker with an uncertainty band (Sehwag to 2026: 214); **the naive ghost** is a pale dotted marker above it (224). The gap between them is shaded and labelled ("the naive way overshoots by about 10, because you cannot scale an outlier up").
  - **Integrity check (release-blocking):** the honest marker's band half-width must be strictly less than the ~10-point gap, so the ghost sits clear above the band and the overshoot is real; QA asserts band < gap on the emitted numbers.
  - **The era dial:** a slider that drags the target year forward or back; each stop is a lookup into the precomputed table (per player-season: raw strike rate, own-era percent-above-par, naive re-quote and honest re-quote per era). **No live model.**
  - **"Find their modern twin":** one tap jumps the era dial to the modern player-season at the same rank, so the reader sees who a teleported legend would be today.
- **Machine B, rank each player against his own era (unit = percent above par), drawn as a bar-SWAP (cognitive-design #6):** after Machine A clears, two panels. Panel 1 (raw strike rate): Fraser-McGurk 2024's bar (234) towers over Gayle 2011's (183). Panel 2 (measured against each era's going rate, with each era's par drawn as the baseline, low-par 2011 vs high-par 2024): the bars physically swap and Gayle's is taller (+56.7% vs +35.4%). The swap carries the "raw says Fraser-McGurk, era-adjusted says Gayle" reversal as a percept, with no subtraction and **no z-score anywhere on the card**; the verdict is scoped to the pairwise ranking ("still edges"), never a superlative over all player-seasons (fallacies-guard S1).
- **The scripted default:** C10-6's scroll opens Machine A's Sehwag ghost-versus-honest beat and, after the hard reset, Machine B's Gayle-versus-Fraser-McGurk bar-swap without any drag, so both beats land for the thumb that only scrolls.
- **Reduced motion:** two static sub-cards, the lift not played (invariant 8): Machine A's ghost and honest markers on one axis with the shaded gap, then Machine B's raw and era-adjusted bars side by side so the swap is a visible still.

## 12. The demoted-exhibit footnotes, in full (the ones the beat budget pushed off the main flow)

### `ch10-microera`, the 2021 venue-leg micro-era (served from C10-3 / C10-4)

**Some "era breaks" are venue-leg composition, kept as a footnote.** When the machine scans the per-match series strictly, it lands a break at 2021, which looks like a mid-pandemic rule shift. It is mostly geography. The 2021 men's season was split across two venues: the India leg scored at 8.41 an over (9,499 runs off 6,777 balls) and the UAE leg, on slower pitches in a bio-bubble, at 7.71 (9,123 off 7,099). So a chunk of the 2021 "break" in the wide rate and the run rate is not a change in how the game was played but a change in WHERE it was played that season. We keep this off the main flow because the hero story is the genuine staggered breaks (2014, 2018, 2022, 2023, 2024), not a venue artefact, but it is the honest footnote to the one break that is partly a mirage. (The technical detail, a strict per-match changepoint at 2021 attributable to leg composition, lives here.)

### `ch10-teleporter`, the non-reproducible-z honesty note (served from C10-6)

**Why the chapter ships a percent gap and never a z-score.** The blueprint teased "how far above his own era" as a z-score of about +5.5 for Gayle 2011 and about +5.2 for Fraser-McGurk 2024. Those numbers did not reproduce on the record: recounting the same idea gives about +3.5 and +3.3, and the exact magnitudes even flip depending on which set of batters you measure against, so a z-score here is method-sensitive and not trustworthy to quote. What DOES hold robustly is the plain reading: measured against the going rate of his own year, Gayle 2011 was 56.7% faster than his era and Fraser-McGurk 2024 was 35.4% faster than his, so Gayle ranks higher even though his raw strike rate is about 51 points lower. So the chapter drops the z entirely and ships the percent-above-par gap on screen, and this footnote records why. (The technical names, SR+ and the era-relative z, live here; the z is named only to explain why it is not used.)
