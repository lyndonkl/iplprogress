## Hard invariants (the header block, every scene inherits these; none may break them)

1. **ONE controlling morph.** Free field to the **1,331 match-dots** (`matchdots`, C8-2), plus its free reverse leg (C8-10). That is the chapter's entire morph budget. Nothing else re-sorts the field.
2. **One held scalar over the morph.** `matchSplit` (C8-3): a 0 to 1 lerp that lifts each held match-dot into its toss lane (bat-first up, field-first down). It is the flowLift analog, NOT a second morph.
3. **One subset-highlight.** The **988 review balls** (C8-4) fly out of the held match-dots into per-team green-and-red chip stacks (the setDismissals/setSparks precedent). It spends no morph.
4. **The 2D annotation-plane scenes.** The belief-reality crossover (C8-3), the spell mosaics (C8-5), the momentum audit panel (C8-6), the required-rate curve (C8-7), and the WPL adoption curves (C8-8) are DOM/SVG over the held (dimmed) match-dots. The payoff (C8-9) is DOM.
5. **Payload budget.** `scenes/ch8.json` plus its inline 16 payoff cards ship inside the **2MB-per-chapter gzip budget**. No new per-point buffer (the match-dots use the `match_bounds` data-texture approach, §6).
6. **Attribute ceiling.** The single BufferGeometry binds every per-point attribute at once. The field is at **14 attributes** and Chapter 8 must keep it there (a 15th attribute blacked out the whole field on one build, per the field integration map). The match-dots therefore add NO per-point attribute: they binary-search a `match_bounds` data texture in-shader.
7. **Reduced motion = live end-state jump-cuts.** Every scene renders its landed state instantly, no motion or tap required. The morph forces t=1; the crossover, mosaics, momentum panel, required-rate curve, adoption curves, and chip stacks render as static end states.
8. **Glossary rule.** No statistical term of art appears in main-flow or teaser copy. Every device carries a fan-language stage name on screen; the technical name lives one click deep in the footnote (§0.2).
9. **The WPL framing rule.** The WPL is never "behind." In Chapter 8 it is **a league born into the analytics age, behaving like one**: it adopts the field-first doctrine on a fast two-season curve, calibrates its reviews like the IPL, and out-fragments the modern men's game. Banned as objects of "behind / lags / catching up / years behind."
10. **Evidence, not proof (the integrity spine).** Every belief is GRADED, never proven. A toss gap is a belief-versus-outcome mismatch, not "the toss causes wins." Momentum is graded against shuffled cricket, never declared "caused" or "debunked" beyond what the shuffle shows. The chapter names beliefs and shows whether the data backs them; it never claims a cause.
11. **The report card is the shape.** Four fails and one pass, the pass saved for last. FAIL (toss), FAIL (reviews), FAIL (spells), FAIL-with-an-honest-residual (momentum), PASS (required rate). The pass exists to make the four fails sting.

### 0.1 Encoding grammar, the match-dots, the toss lanes, the belief-reality crossover, the review chips, the spell mosaics, the momentum panel, the report-card rail (cognitive-design)

- **The match-dot is the new unit, and the reader watches balls BECOME it (the controlling morph).** In `free`, the field is 316,199 balls scattered in the sky. In `matchdots`, every ball condenses to the centroid of the match it belongs to, plus a small radial jitter disc, so **one glowing dot equals one match** and there are **1,331 of them**. The x axis is season/time (2008 at the left, 2026 at the right); the y axis before any split is the match centroid. The morph is the constellation precedent (`posConstellation` / `uMatchDotR`): balls do not cross the wire, they resolve to their match block in-shader. The reader is taught this unit in C8-2 before any argument is made on it, because "a dot made of a whole match" is the easiest mark in the chapter to misread as "a dot is a ball."

- **The toss lanes are a scalar over the held dots, and the swelling river is the data's own shape (`matchSplit`).** In C8-3 the `matchSplit` scalar lerps 0 to 1: at 0 every dot sits on its neutral centroid; at 1 each dot lifts into one of two horizontal lanes by the match's toss class (the winner batted first, upper lane; the winner chose to field first, lower lane). No dot is invented and no lane is padded: the lower lane visibly SWELLS after 2016 because that is when captains started choosing to field, so the "field-first river swelling" is emergent from the real toss column, not an authored animation. Time still runs left to right inside each lane, so the swell is legible as a change over seasons.

- **The belief-reality crossover is a 2D SVG overlay of two lines that CROSS, and it grades a belief, never asserts a cause.** Over the held toss-split dots (C8-3) the scene draws two lines on the annotation plane: **how often captains chose to field** (the field-first share, 42.9% climbing to 77.1%) and **how often the chase actually won** (the batting-second win rate, 54.3% up to a 59.6% hump in 2016-19 then back to 52.8%). These are what the real data draws, so the lines CROSS: in 2008-10 captains chose to field LESS often than the chase actually won (43 below 54); the field-first line then overtakes the chase-win line around the early 2010s and ends far above it (77 versus 53). **The visual is the scissors/crossover, NOT a shaded wedge** (shading the area between two crossing lines makes a misleading bowtie, and a vertical gap between a choice-percent and a result-percent is not a real subtractable quantity, cognitive-design must-fix and fallacies-guard). The two lines are **direct-end-labeled** ("chose to field", "the chase won") and separated by **line style plus luminance, never a green/red legend** (green/red means outcome on the review chips, §0.1, and must not be re-bound here); each line carries a small **kind-tag, "a choice" and "a result"**, and a light divider makes clear they are two different measurements, not two heights to subtract. The **crossing point is annotated** ("here captains started choosing to field more often than chasing actually won"). The chase-win line is drawn as its honest 5-point shape including the 2016-19 hump, so the copy never asserts "flat" over a visibly non-flat line. The crossover is authored entirely in the Ch8 annotations component (the Ch7 license-surface precedent); if it must anchor to match geometry it uses `getMatchDotsLayout()` plus `projectToCss()`. It is a belief-versus-outcome mismatch, not a causal quantity, and the register (§0.2) and the footnote both state that plainly. Reduced motion renders it static.

- **The review chips are a casino table: green means the review paid off, red means the call just stood (the subset).** In C8-4 the 988 review deliveries fly out of the held match-dots into **per-team chip stacks**, one lane per franchise. Inside each lane the chips split into two bands: a **green** band (the on-field call was overturned, the review was right, 292 chips) and a **red** band (the call stood, the review did not overturn anything, 696 chips). The eye reads "mostly red" as "most reviews did not pay off" without any number. **The load-bearing "mostly red" read MUST NOT depend on hue** (green/red is the canonical colorblind-confusion pair, and this is a male-skewed audience, cognitive-design release-blocking must-fix): so the two bands carry **two redundant, colorblind-safe channels** beyond hue, (i) a strong **luminance separation** (the struck-down band is drawn darker, the paid-off band lighter, so "mostly dark = mostly did not pay off" survives with no color at all), and (ii) a **micro-glyph on the chips themselves** (a tick on the paid-off chips, a small cross on the struck-down chips), carrying the report-card rail's cross/tick semantics onto the bands so the read is icon-first, color-second. **The red review-chip color MUST also be visually distinct from any team's own red identity color** (a franchise-red reader looking at their own lane must never confuse "my team" with "the call stood"); the guardrail is enforced three ways: (i) the review-red is a specific luminance-and-hue chip red held apart from every team red in the palette, (ii) the team-glow-mute guardrail extends so that while the chips are engaged every point's identity glow is muted, so identity color cannot bleed into the chip read, and (iii) because that mute removes the reader's own-team color-pop, the reader's own lane is marked at its head by a **non-red, non-glow marker** (a small crest or a bracket) so "look for your team first" (§0.3) still works preattentively. Green is held apart from the WPL teal for the same reason.

- **The spell mosaics are authored SVG bowler-strips, and a spell reads as one fused bar by CONNECTEDNESS, not by color alone.** In C8-5 the scene draws each exemplar innings as a **single ordered 1x20 strip** (one cell per over, left to right in bowling order, up to twenty), colored by its bowler. Crucially, **adjacent same-bowler overs are merged into one fused bar with a visible gap before a different bowler**, so run-length is a direct spatial-length read: a settled spell is literally one long bar, and fragmentation is many separate tiles. Connectedness is the strongest Gestalt grouping cue and is colorblind-robust, so the read does not rest on telling six-to-eight bowler hues apart (a single 1x20 strip also never wraps a run across a row, which a grid would sever). The two eras are shown as **side-by-side (stacked on mobile) small multiples** so the comparison is parallel, not held across a memory gap. The exemplars are **near-median innings for each era, labeled "one example innings" (never "typical")**, and the aggregate share (55 to 64 in 100) sits adjacent so the picture never outruns the number (a hand-picked "confetti" innings would overstate a ~9-point shift as if it were 0 to 100, which a fallacies audit flags). The mark is taught before it is argued: the caption says what a cell is and what a long fused bar means before any share is named.

- **The momentum panel is a needle against shuffled cricket, and the honest residual is a labeled, categorical read on screen.** In C8-6 the scene renders a picked claim's **observed value as a needle** laid over the **histogram of the same claim measured on shuffled cricket** (the deliveries dealt back in a random order, thousands of shuffles, all precomputed at build time in `scenes/ch8.json`). The cloud's mass is drawn as an **explicit shaded band**, and a **"no effect" reference line sits at 1.0** with **"more likely ->" / "<- less likely" side labels** (so a curious tapper never reads a claim that clears the cloud the WRONG way, like the wicket claim at 0.93, as "the belief holds"). If the needle sits inside the band, the pattern is indistinguishable from chance; if it clears the band, something real survives, and **"outside the band" is made a categorical read**: the needle draws **green when it is outside the band, grey when inside**, so a fine +7% distance near the just-noticeable-difference threshold does not have to be eyeballed. Two shuffles are precomputed: a plain shuffle (within an innings and phase) and a fuller shuffle that also holds the batter fixed ("the same batter's own balls, reshuffled"). **On mobile the two clouds are NEVER overlaid; the scene SEQUENCES them** (first the needle against the plain cloud, then that cloud is REPLACED by the same-batter cloud), so only one distribution is on screen at a time and each gets the full phone width (cognitive-design must-fix). The honest residual is never hidden and never overstated: when the fuller shuffle eats most of an edge, the surviving sliver is drawn with a **labeled bracket spanning the band edge to the needle ("the real part: about +7%")**, and that residual is **steady across the eras (about 1.07 season after season), not fading** (the raw edge fades, the real part does not, fallacies-guard); when a claim fails to clear even the plain shuffle it is drawn failing.

- **The report-card rail is the chapter's spine (a persistent DOM overlay, no field cost).** A slim five-slot rail rides one edge of the frame from C8-3 on. As each belief is graded, its slot stamps a verdict: a **cross** for FAIL, a **tick** for PASS, with the fifth slot held blank until C8-7 so the pass is a genuine reveal. The verdict uses an icon first and color second (cross/tick, then red/green), so the grade never depends on color alone (fallacies-guard). The rail makes the "four fails, one pass" shape legible at a glance and gives the chapter its report-card frame without spending a caption on it.

- **One hue-meaning at a time, re-taught every scene (cross-chapter vigilance).** Hue is rebound scene to scene (identity in C8-2, outcome on the review chips in C8-4, bowler id on the spell strips in C8-5), so the glow-mute guardrail keeps only ONE hue-meaning active at a time and **no scene ever shows two hue-meanings at once**: the crossover's two lines are separated by style plus luminance rather than green/red (which stays reserved for the review chips), the rail's fail-red/pass-green is held distinct from the chip red/green and every team red, and each scene's step 1 re-teaches its own color key before arguing on it.

### 0.2 Register, THE BELIEF AUDIT (on-screen copy rules, Ch 8)

The chapter's voice is a **belief audit**: it takes the things captains and pundits believe in the analytics era, holds each up to the full record, and stamps a grade. The register's five guardrails, on screen in every scene:

1. **Grade the belief, never assert the cause (the integrity spine, release-blocking).** The chapter says "the belief that X" and shows whether the data backs it. It never says the toss "causes" wins, never says a spell "makes" the next over cheaper, never says a wicket "brings" the next wicket as a fact. The toss beat is a belief-versus-outcome mismatch; the momentum beat is a test against shuffled cricket; the required-rate beat is a change in behavior, not a change in who wins. "Suspect / causes / because of / makes / brings" as causal assertions are banned in main flow; the honest verbs are "the belief that / captains bet that / the pattern that / graded against / holds up / does not hold up."

2. **The WPL is analytics-native, never behind (the house WPL framing rule, made literal).** C8-8 shows the WPL adopting the field-first doctrine fast (54.5% in its first season to about 100% two seasons later), calibrating its reviews almost exactly like the IPL (30.5% right versus 29.6%), and fragmenting its spells harder than the modern men's game (75.3%). That is framed as **a league born into the analytics age**, arriving with the culture before the history. "Behind / catching up / years behind / developing toward / immature / a different animal" are banned; the WPL is "born into the analytics age," "analytics-native," "the young league that already behaves like one."

3. **Orient every exhibit before you argue with it.** Nobody is asked to read a swelling river, two crossing lines, a wall of chips, a strip of overs, a needle against a cloud, or a flipped curve until the mark has been taught in dead-plain words: what a match-dot is (C8-2), what the two toss lanes mean (C8-3), what a green and a red chip mean (C8-4), what an over-cell and a long fused bar mean (C8-5), what the needle and the cloud mean (C8-6), what the two ends of the curve mean (C8-7). The match-dots get the most patient orientation because "a dot is a whole match" is the easiest mark to misread.

4. **Interaction adds depth, never carries the thesis.** The captain-simulator toggle (C8-3), tapping a franchise's chip lane (C8-4), swapping the mosaic era (C8-5), and picking a different momentum claim from the menu (C8-6) are all optional depth. The scripted scroll lands every point without a tap, and reduced motion carries every beat.

5. **One number per idea; the extras go to the footnote.** Each caption step carries one moving number (a before-and-after pair counts as one idea). Full era ladders, per-season sequences, sample sizes, and the second shuffle's z-scores live in the footnote, never on the main-flow card.

**Banned on screen** (main flow plus teasers), inheriting every prior list plus Ch 8's terms of art: *field-first percent, chase-win percent, win probability, required run rate, run rate (as a bare term of art), economy, permutation test, permutation null, autocorrelation, null hypothesis, shuffled null (as a term of art), p-value, z-score, significance, confidence interval, base rate, conditional probability, lift, odds ratio, DRS (as a bare acronym), umpire's call (as data vocab), upheld, struck down (as bare data vocab), stratify, stratification, confound, confounder, causation, causal, correlation (as a term of art), regression, Bayesian, shrinkage, expected value, diff-in-diff, natural experiment.* Also inherited and still banned as league comparisons: *behind, ahead (of a league), lags, lagging, catching up, N years behind, developing toward, immature, a different animal.* (Note: "ahead of the rate" and "ahead at halfway" describe a chasing team's match position and are ALLOWED; only "ahead/behind" as a league-standing comparison is banned.)

## 1. CHAPTER 8, The Captain's Brain

**Beat-budget check (blueprint report-card shape): 2 heroes + 3 supporting in main flow.**
- **Hero 1 = the toss revolution** (C8-3): the field-first river swells while the chase never pays any better. FAIL.
- **Hero 2 = the review economics** (C8-4): more reviews, a lower hit rate. FAIL, and the honest delta is that the success rate got worse, not merely flat (scoped to the measured share overturned, never "the reviewing got dumber").
- **Supporting 1 = spell fragmentation and the cold-return tax** (C8-5): rhythm captaincy dissolves into matchup chess, and the tax for coming back cold GREW. FAIL.
- **Supporting 2 = momentum** (C8-6): "a wicket brings a wicket" collapses against shuffled cricket, and most of "hitting begets hitting" is just good batters batting, with a thin real sliver left. FAIL with an honest residual.
- **Supporting 3 = required-rate responsiveness** (C8-7): the one belief the data backs, the chase pacing curve genuinely flipped. PASS, saved for last so the four fails sting.

**Framing beats (not counted in the 2+3):** C8-1 title and handoff, C8-2 the controlling morph (orient the match-dots), C8-8 the WPL doctrine transmission (the WPL thread, structural not decorative), C8-9 the per-franchise payoff, C8-10 the chapter close.

## 12. The demoted-exhibit footnotes, in full (the two the beat budget pushed off the main flow)

### `ch8-matchup`, the Matchup Engineering Score and Engine #6 (served from C8-5 and C8-9)

**Lead with the raw material growing, not the score.** The thing that genuinely changed is how much head-to-head history a captain now has to work with. Count, for every ball, whether the batter and bowler had already faced each other at least a dozen deliveries before that moment: it was 12.4% of balls in 2009, 42.1% by 2019, and about 32% after the 2022 expansion diluted the pool with new teams (33.0%, 34.2%, 32.1% across the recent seasons). So the matchup era is real in the sense that the data a captain can consult exploded, more than tripling in a decade.

**The score itself is weak, and we say so.** We asked, for every bowling change, whether the captain picked the bowler with the best record against the batter due to face, judged only on what was known before that ball (no peeking at the future). Captains made 45,276 bowling changes; 42,175 of them (93.2%) had a real choice to engineer (at least two options, at least one with a history). They landed on a best-third-of-the-pool matchup 29.9% of the time against 24.5% by blind luck, about 1.2 times better, and on the single best matchup 27.8% against 22.4%, with an average pool of 4.68 bowlers. That is a weak edge, it shows no adoption ramp over the years, and it is confounded by simpler logic (a captain matching pace to spin, or left-hand to right-hand, would score as "engineering" without ever consulting a head-to-head record). So we lead with the raw material, not the score, and we do not put the score on screen.

**How the head-to-head record is read (Engine #6), one click deeper.** Each pair's record is built with no peeking: matches are ordered by real date, and a ball only ever sees deliveries that came strictly before it in real time (a first-ever meeting shows a blank record, which three snapshot tests confirm on all 33,772 pairs and 305,738 balls, zero violations). Because a thirty-ball sample is noisy, each pair's record is pulled toward the league average until it has earned its weight: the league average is 1.3322 runs a ball, and a pair needs about 51 balls before its own record counts for half, so a sub-thirty-ball matchup sits near par. Balls faced count every delivery except wides. (The technical names, empirical-Bayes shrinkage and the no-lookahead ordering, live here.)

### `ch8-dew`, the Double-Header Dew Ledger (served from C8-3 and C8-8)

Part of why captains choose to field is not belief at all, it is the weather. In day-night matches the evening dew makes the ball skid wet and hard to grip, so bowling second is genuinely harder, which is exactly why a captain who wins the toss puts the other side in and takes the chase. This ledger tracks how much of the field-first surge sits in dew-prone evening slots and double-headers, and it grows with the schedule: as the leagues added more evening and double-header fixtures, the share of tosses played under dew rose, so some of the hardening field-first doctrine is a rational read of the conditions rather than a belief that outran its evidence. We keep this off the main-flow grade because it does not change the verdict (the chase-win rate stayed flat whether or not dew was in play), but it is the honest caveat on Belief 1: not every captain choosing to field is chasing a myth, some are just reading the sky. The WPL, which plays many day-night double-headers, is especially exposed to dew, which is part of why its field-first curve ramped so fast (C8-8).

## 13. The report-card rail and the demand-mode render timeline

### The report-card rail (a persistent DOM overlay, zero field cost)

- **Geometry:** a slim five-slot rail pinned to one edge of the frame (desktop: right edge, vertical, clear of the nav; mobile: a slim top strip, horizontal, clear of the bottom caption). The five slots are the five beliefs in order (toss, reviews, spells, momentum, required rate). It appears from C8-3 on and holds through C8-10's first beat, then fades.
- **Stamp timing:** each slot is blank until its belief is graded, then stamps its verdict at that belief's grade step (C8-3 step 5, C8-4 step 6, C8-5 step 5, C8-6 step 6, C8-7 step 5). The fifth slot (required rate) stays deliberately blank until C8-7 so the PASS is a genuine reveal, not spoiled.
- **Accessibility (fallacies-guard):** the verdict is an ICON first and a color second. FAIL is a cross glyph in a held fail-red; PASS is a tick glyph in a held pass-green. The grade never depends on color alone, and the fail-red is held distinct from both the review-red chip color and every team red. Screen-reader text reads "Belief [n]: [name]. Grade: not backed by the record" for a FAIL (never a negative causal claim like "fielding first loses", note N1), and for the required-rate PASS specifically "Grade: pass, the pacing changed as captains say, not that chasing wins more" (SF4), so the tick is never read as "chasing wins more".
- **No field cost:** the rail is DOM, so it spends no morph, no attribute, and no render window.

The field renders ONLY during these windows; everything else is a provably stopped loop (CONTRACT §8.1 demand mode):
