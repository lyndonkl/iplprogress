### 0.1 Encoding grammar — the constellation, the worm, the threads, the dual dial (cognitive-design)

- **The constellation is a ladder of abstraction run in reverse (the controlling layout `constellation`).** The free field of 316,199 balls condenses, season by season, into **23 glowing stars**: one star per season-group (19 IPL seasons 2008-2026, gi 0-18; 4 WPL seasons 2023-2026, gi 19-22), each star a bright disc of that season's own balls (in-shader deterministic jitter around the star centre, so a star is visibly made of its deliveries, not an abstract dot). The reader watches the whole field *become* the map. Two seasons sit close when they **play alike ball for ball** (their per-ball outcome recipe is similar); far apart when they play differently. The map carries **only closeness**: it has no up or down and no left or right, and even the closeness is a flattened, lossy squeeze of the true ball-for-ball differences, so the chapter's proof lives in two raw numbers computed straight from the data (recipe nearest the men's 2008, rate equal to the men's 2022) and the picture only makes them visible (§0.2 guardrail 6). Positions come from a tiny **per-phase star table** (23 × (x,y), precomputed, §6), read in-shader off `group_ids.u16`; **no positions cross the wire per point.** The data aspect ratio is FIXED (distances between stars are the whole meaning) and the frame **letterboxes** on portrait rather than stretching (the Ch 2/3/4/5 honesty lock), so "the WPL sits beside the men's path" reads identically on desktop and phone. `CONSTELLATION_ASPECT` / `CONSTELLATION_FILL` live in `lib/field/layout.ts`, owner-tunable, flagged for build sign-off.
- **The men's path is a worm; the WPL's kinship is a dotted thread (annotation plane).** The IPL's 19 stars, strung in season order 2008 → 2026, are joined by a glowing **chronological worm** (the men's league's journey through style-space), drawn as SVG registered to the star world coords, **never** GL geometry (the cardinality rule; 18 segments). Each WPL star is joined to its **nearest men's-season lookalike** by a **dotted thread** (its single nearest neighbour by ball-for-ball recipe, per phase, from `scenes/ch6.json`). The worm and the threads are the scene's SVG; the GL field is the 23 star-discs. Hue stays identity: IPL stars in their season-neutral field colour, WPL stars in the WPL family teal, the reader's picked team lit on top (personalization survives into the map).
- **The phase toggle swaps a PRECOMPUTED aligned map, never a live fit.** Powerplay / middle overs / death each have their **own** star table, embedded in the pipeline and then **Procrustes-aligned** (shifted, scaled, turned, and mirrored to best-fit) onto the all-phase master, so a WPL star never jumps to the far side of the men's path just because the reader flipped a phase (the map of distances is the same under any turn or mirror, which is exactly why a browser re-fit is banned: it could pick the mirror image and put the WPL on the wrong side). Flipping the toggle **lerps the star coordinates** between two precomputed tables; every point stays glued to its own season-star and the discs glide a short, coherent distance together (Gestalt common fate per season-cohort). This is **not a second controlling morph and not a re-sort**: the point-to-star assignment never changes, only the 23 star centres move, minimally and Procrustes-locked. It is the exact analog of Ch 5's `pricelens` swapping a color table over the held worth grid, except the constellation's table is positions. (Owner sign-off, open call a: this is the one place a held layout's points move; §4 asserts no WPL star crosses the men's worm under any phase table, and no season-cohort's centroid travels more than `CONSTELLATION_PHASE_MAX` between tables.)
- **The maturity clock is a dual dial (annotation plane, 2D).** Two concentric season dials the reader rotates to snap **both leagues onto the same league-year** (IPL year 1 = 2008, WPL year 1 = 2023); a metric readout re-quotes as the dials turn. The field holds the constellation dimmed behind it; the dial is SVG/DOM. Reduced motion degrades the dial to **preset league-year buttons** (year 1 … year 4), each a static state.
- **The run recipe is two bars, not a live helix (annotation plane, 2D).** The "DNA" is drawn honestly as **two stacked run-composition bars** side by side (what share of a league's RUNS comes from each outcome: singles-and-twos, fours, sixes), IPL vs WPL, with the **four band** and the **six band** called out. The blueprint's "double-helix" is the mood, not the mark; the mark is a plain paired bar so the four-led-vs-six-led contrast is legible in one read. The particles hold the constellation behind it.
- **Working-memory cap holds:** ≤3 numbers per caption step, one on-screen change per step. Any beat that places the WPL on the men's timeline names, in an adjacent step of the SAME scene, a dialect stat that refuses the timeline (the no-single-clock corollary, §2), so the two clocks are always felt together.
- **Back-half novel-encoding discipline (Ware/Mayer).** The chapter's back half introduces four new marks in four scenes (the dual dial C6-6, the run-recipe bars C6-7, the stumping season strips C6-8, the closeness beeswarm C6-9) on top of the map / worm / threads. To keep that load payable, the held constellation stays behind every one for continuity, and each exhibit's **step 1 is pure, glanceable orientation** (what the marks are, what the axis means) before any number. The stumping strips (a per-season rate, league beside league) and the beeswarm (every match on one close-to-blowout axis) deliberately keep DISTINCT marks because they answer different questions, but both open on one dead-plain orientation line so the reader never meets a number before the grammar.

### 0.2 Register — TWO DIALECTS (on-screen copy rules, Ch 6)

Ch 1 was the thesis, Ch 2 mourned the anchor, Ch 3 admired the bowler, Ch 4 stood back as the water rose, the Net Session put the dials in your hands, Ch 5 put a price on every ball. Ch 6 is **the chapter that changes the question**: for five chapters the WPL has ridden along as a second voice in every beat; here it stops being a comparison and becomes a **character in its own right**, a different dialect of the same game. Six guardrails, all load-bearing:

1. **Beside, never behind.** This is the whole chapter and the house framing made literal (§2). The WPL is never "N years behind," never "catching up," never "still developing toward" the men's game. It plays a **different** cricket that happens to score at the same rate. Every sentence that could read as a deficit is rewritten as a dialect difference or an open question about a young league. "Beside the path, not on it" is the thesis; the PROOF is two raw numbers computed straight from the data, ball for ball (the WPL's recipe is nearest the men's 2008, and its rate equals the men's 2022), and the constellation ILLUSTRATES that proof, it does not carry it (§0.2 guardrail 6); "behind / ahead / lagging / catching up" are banned on screen.
2. **Two truths at once, in one breath.** The chapter's spine is a both-and, not an either-or: *the WPL scores at the men's 2022 rate AND plays the ball-for-ball game of the men's 2008.* Neither half is allowed to appear without the other in the same beat (the no-single-clock corollary at chapter scale). The maturity clock (a league-age reading) is always paired with a dialect reading that no clock can hold. The load-bearing "beside" proof is the **simultaneity itself**: a 2008 outcome mix at a 2022 scoring rate is a pairing no single men's season has ever had, so the WPL cannot be any one point on the men's line. Several later beats note the WPL resembling the early men's game (recipe nearest 2008, four-led, spin-led); that is NOT it being an earlier version, precisely because it does all of it at a modern scoring rate, and the genuinely native trait (the tightest finishes in the data) is given equal weight so repetition never quietly re-establishes "earlier version".
3. **Orient the map before you argue with it.** Nobody may be asked "look how the WPL sits beside the path" until the map has been taught in dead-plain words: what a star is (one season of one league), what "close" means (they play alike, ball for ball), and which stars are which league. The constellation is the most abstract image in the whole piece; it gets the most patient orientation.
4. **Honest thin ground, loudly.** The WPL is 88 matches and four seasons. Where a WPL read is young, the copy says so and frames it as promise, not deficit (the payoff card's short-history state is the template). No smoothing artifact is ever presented as a finding; the map is a lookup, not a guess, and the footnote says exactly how it was built.
5. **The dialect is specific, not vibes.** "A different dialect" is cashed out in concrete, verified differences: it scores along the ground (four-led), it leans on spin (the stumping fingerprint), it finishes tighter than the men's game in either era. Each supporting beat is one such difference, named plainly, one number.
6. **The map illustrates; the numbers prove (the MDS-honesty lock).** The constellation is a flattened picture. Closeness between two stars is the ONLY thing it means: up, down, left, and right carry no meaning, and even the closeness is a faithful but lossy squeeze of the true ball-for-ball differences. So no caption ever reads a direction or a heading on the map, and the "beside, not on" finding is grounded in raw numbers computed straight from the data (the WPL's recipe is nearest the men's 2008; its rate equals the men's 2022), never in a position on the picture. The map makes the finding visible; the numbers ARE the finding, and the footnote says so in plain words.

Banned on screen (main flow + teasers), inheriting every prior list plus Ch 6's terms of art: *behind, ahead, lags, lagging, catching up, N years behind, developing toward, immature, distribution, divergence, Jensen-Shannon, embedding, MDS, multidimensional scaling, eigenvector, Procrustes, projection, tensor, Gini, HHI, Lorenz, normalized, z-score, percentile (as a term of art), concentration, variance, cluster (as a verb of art), correlation.* Stage names used on screen in Ch 6:

### Scene C6-3 — The men's path (string the IPL stars on their worm)

**Purpose (one point):** the men's nineteen seasons are not a random scatter; in order, they trace one long journey through style-space, and that journey has a direction.

**Caption steps (one change each):**
1. *(the worm draws 2008 → 2026)* > Join the men's seasons up in order and you get this line. Call it the worm. The map is not a calendar, though. The worm is just the seasons joined in the order they were played, a path traced across a map of who plays like who.

## 5. Copy / design decisions made here (the blueprint left these open) — for owner sign-off at the R4a review

5. **The maturity clock never ships as a single clock** (§0.2 guardrail 2, house corollary): C6-6 pairs the year-4-vs-year-15 read with the 2022-rate-2008-game refusal in the same scene, so the recurring device gets its home chapter without ever placing the WPL "behind".

7. **The "DNA" is drawn as two run-composition bars, not a literal double helix** (§0.1): the helix is the mood, the paired bar is the mark, chosen so the four-led-vs-six-led contrast lands in one read; the full outcome split is one click deep.
