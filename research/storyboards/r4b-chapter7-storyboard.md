### 0.1 Encoding grammar — the twin rivers, the license surface, the placebo cursor, the playbook slope (cognitive-design)

- **The twin rivers are two flowing ribbons of the actual deliveries, and HEIGHT is a labelled run rate, not a pile of balls (the controlling layout `flow`).** The free field of 316,199 balls pours into **two ribbons**: one per league (IPL, WPL family teal), flowing left to right along a **labelled time axis** (season). Each ribbon's height on a **labelled vertical axis, "runs an over,"** is that league's run rate that season; the ribbon has a **fixed decorative thickness** (a constant band around the centreline, NOT a volume encoding), so a season with more balls does not read as a taller or wider river. This is the whole point of the must-fix on encoding-collision: the mark is literally made of deliveries, so unless the axis is labelled and the thickness is held constant, a reader will read "more balls = taller." Here height means run rate, full stop, and the causal read is the **vertical gap between the two centrelines**, which resolves to (and is backed by) a **clean two-line read against that axis** so "the men's river climbs, the women's stays level" is a precise position read, not a vibe. Per-ball position = its league-season river cell (x = season along the ribbon, y = the season's run rate on the axis, plus deterministic in-shader jitter inside the fixed-thickness band so the ribbon is visibly made of its own balls), read in-shader off `group_ids.u16` (season-group incl. the league bit) against a small **river table** (per league-season centreline height, precomputed in `scenes/ch7.json`). The data aspect is FIXED and the frame **letterboxes** on portrait rather than stretching (the Ch 2/3/4/5/6 honesty lock), so the gap reads identically on desktop and phone. `FLOW_ASPECT` / `FLOW_FILL` live in `lib/field/layout.ts`, owner-tunable, flagged for build sign-off.
- **The WPL river is a young tributary that SOURCES at 2023 (the honest diff-in-diff geometry).** The WPL did not exist before 2023, so there is no shared pre-rule stretch where both rivers run side by side. The image is therefore honest by construction: the **IPL ribbon flows alone from 2008**, wandering flat through fifteen seasons; the **WPL ribbon springs into being in 2023**, right alongside the IPL at about the same height; and from that shared 2023 origin the two **fork**, the IPL climbing while the WPL stays nearly level. The "parallel then diverge" the argument needs is carried by (a) the IPL's own long flat fifteen-year baseline (proof the climb is not a pre-existing trend) and (b) the two ribbons forking at the rule year, one steep, one flat. The footnote states plainly that the WPL has no "before," so the evidence is the IPL's own past cross-checked against a rule-free league staying calm over the same four years (§0.2 guardrail 1).
- **The impact-sub sparks are a subset-highlight, not a second morph.** The **556 impact subs** (the twelfth players brought on since 2023, marked on the **517 deliveries** that carry them, IPL only, WPL zero) glow as bright sparks entering the IPL ribbon at their season, so "the rule is made of real substitutions" is literally on the field. This is a luminance/subset lift over the held `flow` layout (the CONTRACT §16 subset pattern), it spends NO second controlling morph, and it is a no-op in every prior scene. The spark set is a small index list in `scenes/ch7.json` (517 point indices), not a new per-point buffer.
- **The license surface is a small colour grid, oriented before it is read (annotation plane, 2D).** "License" is drawn as a **grid of match states**: rows = how many wickets are gone, columns = how many overs are left; each cell's colour = **how hard batters swing there** (their scoring rate). Two grids sit side by side, **before the rule** and **after**, with a **difference grid** that glows in the ONE pocket the License Index names (≥4 wickets gone, the middle overs, glossed "several down, chasing hard"). The License number is **fused into the surface** (it is the reading of that one glowing pocket), not a separate beat, per the should-fix. The particles hold the twin rivers dimmed behind it. Colour is a low-accuracy channel (Cleveland-McGill), so the beat directs the eye to a **single focal pocket** and prints its number rather than asking the reader to read the whole matrix.
- **The placebo cursor is a lookup, and it says so (annotation plane, 2D).** A **draggable marker** rides a labelled season timeline; the readout under it is **"the size of the scoring jump if the rule had come in this year instead."** Behind the marker sits a **grey cloud** of every candidate year's jump ("every other year you could have blamed"). The jump for every candidate year is **precomputed in the pipeline** (a table in `scenes/ch7.json`), so dragging LOOKS UP a value and **never fits a model in the browser** (stated on screen: "a lookup, not a guess"). The scripted scroll path makes the point first (the rule year and the seasons right after stand clear of the grey cloud; every year before the rule is flat); dragging is optional depth (§0.2 guardrail 4). Reduced motion degrades to the true-year value drawn with the grey cloud rendered statically around it.
- **The playbook is a two-state slopegraph, one number moving (annotation plane, 2D).** The full five-stage decision Sankey (toss → innings → timing → role → outcome) is **demoted to the footnote and the sandbox** (must-fix: it is unreadable one-thumb and smuggles a second point). The main-flow beat carries ONE simple trend: the share of impact subs **used at the innings break** versus **held back for a mid-innings strike**, across the seasons, as a **slopegraph** (over half at the break in 2023 sliding down to about a third by 2025). One point, one number moving.
- **Working-memory cap holds:** ≤3 numbers per caption step, one on-screen change per step. Any beat that places the WPL beside the IPL's run-rate timeline names, in an adjacent step of the SAME scene, why the WPL is the ruler and not the laggard (the no-single-clock corollary and the WPL framing rule, §2), so the reader never leaves a beat with "the women's league is just slower."
- **Back-half novel-encoding discipline (Ware/Mayer).** Ch 7 introduces four new marks (the twin rivers, the license grid, the placebo timeline, the playbook slope). To keep that load payable, the held twin rivers stay dimmed behind every 2D exhibit for continuity, and each exhibit's **step 1 is pure, glanceable orientation** (what the ribbons/grid/timeline/slope are, what each axis means) before any number, exactly as Ch 6's back half did.

### 0.2 Register — THE SUSPECT (on-screen copy rules, Ch 7)

Ch 4 showed you the water rise and then, on screen, refused to explain it: *"Something snapped in 2023. Hold that thought, the answer is three chapters away."* Ch 7 is where the detective story pays its first instalment: **here is the prime suspect, and here is how we weigh the evidence without overcharging it.** The suspect is a rule, the Impact Player, the twelfth man teams got in 2023. The evidence is a **control group**: a second league that never adopted the rule. The whole chapter is the discipline of saying "strongest suspect" where a lesser piece would say "cause." Six guardrails, all load-bearing:

1. **Evidence weight, not proof.** This is the chapter and the piece's integrity spine. The rule is the **strongest suspect**, never "the reason," never "what broke the record." Every causal sentence is bounded in the same breath: the rule *opened the door*, but pitches and balls changed too, and whether it was the same players or new ones is still open (Ch 10 settles it). The confound lives in **main-flow copy beside the twin-rivers reveal**, not buried in a footnote. And because the WPL was born the same year as the rule, the design leans on the IPL's own flat fifteen-year past PLUS a rule-free league staying calm over the same four years, a point the footnote states plainly rather than implying a clean before-and-after for both leagues. Banned on screen: *caused, the cause, broke the record, proves, because of the rule* (as a bare claim). Allowed: *the strongest suspect, the rule opened the door, the prime suspect, evidence points at.*
2. **The WPL is the ruler, not the laggard (the house WPL framing rule, made literal).** The twin rivers show the men's river climbing and the women's staying level. That is exactly the shape the WPL framing rule polices, so it is defused **in the same beat**: the WPL is not the slow league here, it is the **yardstick**, and its steady, rule-free path is the whole reason the IPL's jump can be measured at all. "Behind / catching up / years behind / developing toward" are banned; the WPL is "the control group," "the yardstick," "the ruler," "the league that never took the rule."
3. **Orient every exhibit before you argue with it.** Nobody is asked to read a divergence, a glowing pocket, a placebo cloud, or a slope until the mark has been taught in dead-plain words: what the two ribbons are and what their height means (C7-2), what the grid's rows and columns and colour mean (C7-5), what the draggable marker and the grey cloud mean (C7-6), what the two ends of the slope mean (C7-7). The twin rivers get the most patient orientation because height-made-of-balls is the easiest mark in the chapter to misread.
4. **Interaction adds depth, never carries the thesis.** The placebo cursor is the chapter's signature interactive, but the scripted scroll path makes the "the rule year is special" point on its own; dragging is exploration for the reader who wants to prove it to themselves. Nobody is gated on dragging, and the caption lands the point for the thumb that only scrolls.
5. **A lookup, never a guess.** The placebo jump for every year, the river heights, and the license surface are all precomputed roll-ups from the corpus. The cursor says "a lookup, not a guess" on screen. No client-side fit exists to flip a sign or invent a discontinuity.
6. **One number per idea.** The hero teaser anchors on ONE contrast (flat near 8 and a half an over for fifteen years, then up every season to nearly 10). The full sequence (8.99, 9.56, 9.63, 9.88) lives in the footnote. Every conditioned number (the license jump, the top-order slice, the timing share) leads with its insight, not its digits.

Banned on screen (main flow + teasers), inheriting every prior list plus Ch 7's terms of art: *diff-in-diff, difference-in-differences, natural experiment (as a bare term), intervention, intervention date, treatment, treatment date, discontinuity, regression discontinuity, interrupted time series, event study, placebo (as a bare term), counterfactual, confound, confounder, secular trend, parallel trends, entropy, standard error, t-statistic, significance, causal estimate, point estimate, elasticity, coefficient, distribution (as a term of art).* Also inherited and still banned: *behind, ahead, lags, lagging, catching up, N years behind, developing toward, immature, a different animal.* Stage names used on screen in Ch 7:
